# Code of Conduct

This is a small hobby project maintained by **a single person**, but everyone
who shows up here - to file an issue, open a PR, or just ask a question -
deserves to be treated with respect. The rules are short and mostly common
sense.

## The short version

- **Be kind.** Assume good faith. People come from different backgrounds, time
  zones, and skill levels.
- **Be patient.** There's no team and no guaranteed response window. Fixes and
  replies happen when time allows.
- **Keep it constructive.** Criticise ideas and code, not people. "This is
  wrong because X" is fine; insults are not.
- **No harassment.** No personal attacks, discriminatory language, sexualised
  content, or sustained disruption. This applies in issues, PRs, discussions,
  and any other project space.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces - the issue tracker, pull requests,
discussions, and anywhere someone is representing the project in public.

## Enforcement

If someone is being a problem, I'll deal with it. Depending on severity that
can mean a warning, having comments edited or removed, or being blocked from
the repository.

To report behaviour that breaks this code, use GitHub's
[report abuse](https://docs.github.com/en/communities/maintaining-your-safety-on-github/reporting-abuse-or-spam)
flow, or open a minimal issue asking me to open a private channel - **without**
restating the offending content in public. Reports are handled confidentially
and in good faith.
